## Research

#### Technosignatures
I am currently performing research as a member of the [PSETI Center](https://pseti.psu.edu) research. With my advisor Jason Wright, I study "technosignatures," or signs of technology made by extraterrestrial life. Ongoing projects are:
- I have recently submitted a paper where I used MESA to model the evolutionary and observable consequences of Dyson sphere feedback on stellar structure and evolution.
- I am working with a small group to ready a SETI class project about the solar gravitational lens for journal submission.

#### Past Projects
For my second-year research project and comprehensive exam project, I worked with Kevin Luhman to characterize the low-mass star and brown dwarf initial mass function in the W3 star-forming complex. Our [paper](https://ui.adsabs.harvard.edu/abs/2021AJ....161..138H/abstract) has been published in the Astronomical Journal.

As an undergraduate at The Ohio State University, I designed a population synthesis model of the Milky Way in Python, advised by Matthew Penny and Scott Gaudi. The model was designed and illustrated to calculate microlensing observables in preparation for microlensing surveys like the Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope (formerly WFIRST).
